# SFI Atlas Field Mark

## Decision

The mark provided in the ATLAS exterior system is not a decorative placeholder. It should be registered as the canonical **SFI Atlas Field Mark**.

It is not a generic corporate logo. It is the field seal used with the wordmark **SYSTEM FRICTION INSTITUTE**.

## Canonical structure

- Outer circle: field boundary.
- Inner dashed circle: latent core / unresolved nucleus.
- Vertical dashed axis: longitudinal observation.
- Curved solid trajectory: observed relation.
- Curved dashed trajectory: inferred or latent relation.
- Five nodes: evidence points distributed through the field.
- Central node: active observation point.

## Usage

Use the mark:

- above or near the wordmark on Atlas-facing surfaces;
- on Field Briefs;
- on observatory transition states;
- on evidence seals;
- on physical artefact systems.

Do not use the mark:

- as a mascot;
- as a decorative repeated pattern;
- as a social icon detached from SFI;
- as a replacement for evidence;
- as a claim of authority without source state.

## Wordmark relationship

The correct public identity is:

`[Atlas Field Mark]`

`SYSTEM FRICTION INSTITUTE`

The mark and the wordmark form the seal. The mark alone may be used only in constrained spaces where the surrounding context already identifies SFI.

## Material rule

The physical system remains primary:

- ATLAS: black hard cover, soft-touch or black cloth, subtle topology, serial spine.
- Cuadernillo: A5 technical B&W companion.
- Sobre Negro: A4/C4 matte black evidence archive.
- Slipcase: housing exterior, longitudinal observatory.

Rule: nothing exterior should sell. Everything exterior should contain pressure.
