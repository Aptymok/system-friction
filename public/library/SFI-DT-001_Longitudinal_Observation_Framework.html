PHENOTYPE

Definition

A Fenotype of Friction is a relatively stable observable pattern of response under minimal perturbation. It is inferred from language, delay, bodily signal, contradiction, field response and post-intervention trajectory.

A phenotyping statement never says what a person is. It describes how a friction pattern is currently organized.

LIMITATIONS

Limitations

This instrument is not a clinical diagnosis, medical evaluation or substitute for professional mental health care. Its function is operational and research-oriented: to record friction patterns, formulate hypotheses and test minimal perturbations longitudinally.

All values, classifications and phenotypes in early cases must be treated as provisional until the Atlas contains enough contrast to evaluate predictive reliability.
